About the League

The Tim McCabe Memorial Cup has been running since 2007, making it one of the longest-running fantasy hockey leagues among its members. Named in honour of Tim McCabe, the league brings together the same core group of friends each season for head-to-head hockey competition. Over 19 seasons it has produced memorable champions, contentious trades, and plenty of trash talk.

Rules

Format

Head-to-head points league. Weekly matchups run throughout the NHL regular season. Teams are scored on a set of offensive and goaltending statistical categories each week.

Buy-In & Payouts

Each manager pays a buy-in at the start of the season. Buy-in has increased over the years: $50 (2017–2018), $100 (2019–2021), $125 (2022–2024), $150 (2025–2026).

Payout structure (based on playoff finish, 10 teams):
1st place — collects the buy-in from every manager who finished 5th or worse
2nd place — collects the buy-in from the 4th-place finisher
3rd place — gets their own buy-in refunded (free season)
4th place — pays their buy-in to 2nd place
5th–10th — each pays their buy-in to 1st place

Net at $150 buy-in: 1st +$900, 2nd +$150, 3rd $0, 4th–10th −$150.
See the Finances tab for historical buy-ins and full payout records.

President's Trophy

Awarded to the manager with the best record at the end of the regular season (before playoffs begin). A separate recognition from the championship — you can win the President's Trophy and still lose in the playoffs. The Tritty Tax payer sends $50 to the President's Trophy winner each season.

Tritty Tax

The manager who finishes last in the regular season standings — before playoffs begin — must pay the Tritty Tax: $50 sent to the President's Trophy winner. Playoff results do not affect who pays; it is determined solely by regular season record. Named after Tristan Melton, who paid it two years running (2018–2019). It has since been referred to as the Melton Tax in his honour.

Playoffs

The top 6 teams qualify for the championship bracket. Seeds 1 and 2 receive a first-round bye; seeds 3–6 play in the first round.

The bottom 4 teams (7th–10th) compete in a consolation bracket for the 1st overall pick in the following season's draft. The first two teams eliminated from the championship bracket play each other for the 5th overall pick.

Draft

Traditional (non-snake) format draft. Each manager picks once per round in the same fixed order.

Draft order is determined by the previous season's playoff finish:
Pick 1 — 7th place
Pick 2 — 8th place
Pick 3 — 9th place
Pick 4 — 10th place
Pick 5 — 5th place (consolation bracket winner)
Pick 6 — 6th place
Pick 7 — 4th place
Pick 8 — 3rd place
Pick 9 — 2nd place
Pick 10 — 1st place (champion picks last)

Trade Deadline

In-season trades close at the Yahoo-set trade deadline. No in-season trades are permitted after that point. Specific deadline week varies by season.

Off-season trades are permitted outside of Yahoo. Managers agree on trades via the group chat and notify the league before the deal is finalised. The off-season trade deadline is 2 weeks before the draft date.

Waivers

Waiver Type: Free Agent Acquisition Budget (FAB) with a continual rolling list tiebreak.
Waiver Mode: Standard.
Waiver Period: 2 days.

Scoring Categories

Skaters: Goals (G), Assists (A), Power Play Points (PPP), Shots on Goal (SOG), Plus/Minus (+/−), PIM, Faceoff Wins (FW).
Goalies: Wins (W), GAA, Save % (SV%), Shutouts (SO).
